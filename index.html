About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Statistics at the University of Minnesota, advised by Prof. Aaron J. Molstad. My research develops interpretable, scalable, and reproducible statistical methods for high-dimensional unsupervised learning, with a primary focus on convex clustering, regularization, and optimization-based model design.

Research

My work studies how different classes of structured penalties—such as sparsity-inducing, fusion-based, and geometry-aware regularizers—shape clustering behavior, interpretability, and statistical stability. I build statistically principled formulations supported by reliable optimization algorithms, spanning convex, smooth, and nonsmooth frameworks. Although many of my projects are motivated by spatial transcriptomics, multi-omic, microbiome, and other complex biological datasets, the methods are designed to be broadly applicable across modern high-dimensional settings.
