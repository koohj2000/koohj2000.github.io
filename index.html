Ph.D. Candidate in Statistics
School of Statistics, University of Minnesota – Twin Cities

Research Publications Teaching Awards Service

I develop interpretable, scalable, and reproducible statistical methods for high-dimensional unsupervised learning, with a focus on convex clustering, regularization, and optimization-based model design. My work examines how structured penalties shape clustering behavior and statistical stability, and is motivated by applications across spatial transcriptomics, multi-omic, microbiome, and other complex biological datasets. I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Aaron J. Molstad, whose guidance has shaped much of my work in convex clustering and high‑dimensional statistical methodology.
